Why do we call it Halloween?

November 1 is All Saint’s Day in the Christian church. October 31 was known as All Hallows E’en, which was shortened to Halloween.

When did Halloween start?

The holiday traces back to a Celtic festival called Samhain. It celebrated the end of the harvest and kicked off the new year. People believed it was when the veil between the worlds of the living and the dead was at its thinnest, and spirits roamed the land.

Why do we carve pumpkins?

The Irish carved scary faces onto turnips to frighten away ghouls. In America, the tradition lives on with pumpkins instead of turnips. The name jack-o'-lantern comes from a story of a clever man who tricked the devil out of collecting his soul.

Why do we trick or treat?

In Britain, poor people went begging, receiving treats called “soul cakes” in exchange for a promise to pray for the dead. Americans adopted the tradition, asking for candy instead.

Why do we dress up in costumes?

By 1000 A.D., Christians honored the dead on All Souls' Day, dressing up as saints, angels, and devils.
